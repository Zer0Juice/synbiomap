The project should be organized around three escalating claims of increasing generality: first, that the semantic embedding approach is internally valid (parts are close to their source papers in embedding space); second, that iGEM and academic research co-specialize geographically in cities with sufficient data depth; and third, that this co-specialization is plausibly structured in time, with iGEM activity preceding or accompanying academic and patent activity. The carbon capture case study should serve as a validation scaffold and running illustration throughout, not an afterthought. Outside scope: BLAST sequence matching, team collaboration networks, and strong causal claims — the project should consistently use language of semantic relatedness and association, and treat the temporal analysis as exploratory. The most urgent empirical task is checking how many cities have enough data across all three artifact types to support the geographic analysis at all.
